Freeze Periods

● IBM Corporate Change Freeze Community - The purpose of this community is to facilitate communication about the freezes and the exception process that is used to manage the freezes.

● Change Freeze Schedule - IBM has extremely critical business periods at the end of each quarter. The IBM Office of the CIO declares Corporate Freezes to protect key IBM business operations during these periods. There is a Full Corporate Freeze, which affects all changes to applications, services, and infrastructure components. In addition there are three limited scope freezes designed to protect specific areas of the business during the times most critical to them.

Single Sign On

 ● Web Identity Single Sing On (SSO) Process  - This document describes the process to deploy applications that require Web Identity Single Sign-On access, W3 SSO (internal facing websites) or WWW SSO (external facing websites). For the purpose of this document both of these environments will be referred to as Single Sign On or (SSO). 
Link: notes://D01DBM03/85256F39004DF3B2/4B87A6F6EAEAADD385256A2D006A582C/D2E4D7720957A2F2852570540056DBA4
● Web Identity (www) SSO boarding - Overview of the Web Identity (www) SSO boarding process 
● w3 SSO boarding - Overview of the w3 SSO boarding process 
● Federated SSO - The federated SSO solution provides improved security and simplifies end user access to applications that require user authentication. It improves security by providing internal users the ability to authenticate with w3 and still be able to access protected resources in the www.ibm.com domain. Internal users no longer need to enter their w3 authentication credentials on an internet page. In addition, participation in the SSO environment by your application will require users to log on in fewer places. Finally, any IBM IDs that are linked to IBM Intranet IDs will be automatically revoked when users are removed form the internal directory (usually when a user leaves the company).
